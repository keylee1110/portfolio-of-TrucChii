Create a premium editorial testimonial section inspired by a scattered floating layout.

Overall direction:
The section should feel like a dark, cinematic portfolio testimonial area, not a standard SaaS testimonial grid. Use a black background, oversized typography, floating testimonial cards, thin blue guide lines, and lots of negative space.

Section structure:
- Full-width black section.
- At the top, add an oversized heading that is partially cropped horizontally by the viewport.
- Heading text example: “Testimonial© - Reviews”
- The heading should be very large, bold, white, and slightly overflowing outside the container to create an editorial feel.
- Under the heading, center a small pill button that says “GET IN TOUCH”.
- Below the button, place a small circular icon/avatar button in the center.
- Add a thin horizontal blue line across the full section width around the upper-middle area.

Testimonial card layout:
- Do not use a normal 3-column grid.
- Cards must be positioned asymmetrically across the section, like floating notes on a large canvas.
- Use absolute positioning or CSS grid with manual placement.
- Each card should have different x/y positions to create a scattered composition.
- Some cards can be partially near the edge of the screen.
- Keep lots of empty black space between cards.

Card style:
- Dark card background, close to #050505 or #080808.
- Thin border, around 1px, using dark gray with subtle blue highlight.
- Rounded corners around 6px to 10px.
- Small compact size, around 220px to 260px wide.
- Padding around 14px to 18px.
- Text should be white or light gray.
- Quote text should be small, dense, and professional.
- Bottom area includes avatar, name, role, and client logo.
- Add a small blue verified badge next to the name.
- Logo should sit on the bottom right of the card.

Composition:
- Card 1: left side, just below the blue horizontal line.
- Card 2: right side, slightly lower than card 1.
- Card 3: center lower area.
- Card 4: far right lower area, partially lower in the viewport.
- Optional card 5: left bottom area, partly cropped to create depth.

Animation:
- On scroll, cards should fade in and move slightly upward.
- Each card should have a different delay.
- Add subtle parallax movement when scrolling.
- On hover, each card should lift slightly, border becomes brighter, and background becomes a bit lighter.
- Keep animation minimal, premium, and smooth.

Responsive:
- On desktop, keep the scattered absolute layout.
- On tablet, reduce heading size and bring cards slightly closer.
- On mobile, switch cards into a vertical stacked layout, because absolute scattered layout may become unreadable.
