

## Prompt to Restyle the Entire Website

**Redesign and restyle the entire website from scratch.**
The current design looks too much like a **generic digital marketing agency / SaaS landing page**, with bold orange accents, dashboard-like visuals, conversion-focused CTA styling, and a commercial hero-section aesthetic.

I want the **entire website** to be transformed into a **premium black-and-white editorial portfolio**, visually much closer to a **high-end creative portfolio** like the reference style.

This is **not** a startup website, **not** a performance dashboard product site, and **not** a typical agency landing page.

It should be a **personal portfolio website for a digital marketing specialist**, with a strong emphasis on:

* campaign visuals
* marketing case studies
* measurable results
* content performance
* social media growth
* campaign archive
* strategy and creativity

However, all of that content must be presented in a **luxury editorial portfolio aesthetic**, not a corporate marketing style.

---

## Global Visual Direction

Apply this style to the **entire website**, not just the hero section:

* dominant **black background**
* white, off-white, and gray typography only
* no bright accent colors as primary styling
* if needed, use only a **very subtle muted accent**, but the site should remain almost fully monochrome
* oversized bold editorial typography
* thin divider lines between sections
* strong negative space
* asymmetrical but highly curated composition
* cinematic image treatment
* minimalist and premium layout
* a feeling of **fashion editorial meets creative portfolio**
* refined, sharp, modern, and intentional

The site should feel like a **luxury personal portfolio** for a campaign strategist / digital marketer.

---

## What to Remove from the Current Style

Please remove or avoid all of the following across the whole website:

* bright orange as a dominant accent
* colorful CTA buttons
* SaaS-like cards
* dashboard-heavy visuals
* UI that looks like analytics software
* startup-style landing page structure
* generic agency design patterns
* overly sales-driven sections
* overly corporate marketing layouts
* boxed KPI cards that feel like a software admin dashboard
* visuals that make the site look like a product website instead of a personal portfolio

---

## What the New Style Should Feel Like

The entire website should feel closer to:

* a **creative director portfolio**
* a **luxury editorial digital portfolio**
* a **minimal black-and-white visual identity**
* a **curated campaign archive**
* a **strategic marketing portfolio with artistic presentation**

The website should communicate:

**“This person is a digital marketer who creates campaigns, shapes content, and delivers measurable growth - but their portfolio is presented with high-end visual taste and editorial precision.”**

---

## New Art Direction for All Sections

### 1. Header / Navigation

Keep the top header extremely minimal and refined.

* left: personal name / logo, e.g. **TrucLu™**
* center: small navigation links
* right: role and location in subtle small uppercase text

Typography should be restrained, clean, and elegant.

---

### 2. Hero Section

Redesign the hero to feel **editorial and personal**, not commercial.

Use:

* oversized bold typography
* black-and-white imagery
* asymmetrical composition
* minimal outlined CTA buttons
* subtle metrics integrated as small text labels
* campaign-related visual collage instead of a dashboard screenshot

The hero should introduce the person as a **digital marketing strategist** with both creativity and measurable performance, but in a **portfolio aesthetic**, not a sales-page aesthetic.

---

### 3. About Section

Make the about section feel like a **personal profile spread**.

Use:

* large portrait or personal branding image
* elegant typography
* concise positioning statement
* subtle metric snippets
* clean spacing and thin divider lines

This section should feel editorial, like a personal introduction in a portfolio book.

---

### 4. Campaigns / Featured Works

This should become a **curated showcase**, not a grid of normal marketing cards.

Use:

* asymmetrical project layout
* large campaign visuals
* editorial image cropping
* project numbering like **(01), (02), (03)**
* campaign names, platforms, goals, and results displayed elegantly
* layered or overlapping images when appropriate
* subtle small text annotations

The campaign section should feel like **featured creative works**, even though they are marketing campaigns.

---

### 5. Results / Metrics Section

The current metrics style should be changed from “dashboard UI” to **editorial information design**.

Instead of software-style KPI cards, use:

* large bold metric typography
* spacious layout
* clean statistic blocks
* minimal labels
* refined visual rhythm
* elegant text hierarchy

The numbers should feel like a **designed performance spread**, not a web app dashboard.

---

### 6. Case Study Section

Redesign this section as a **premium long-form case study layout**.

Use:

* large section title
* strong typography hierarchy
* campaign visuals mixed with text
* annotation-like data points
* elegant storytelling structure
* spacious composition

It should feel like a **design case study / campaign editorial**, not a blog article or business report.

---

### 7. Expertise / Services Section

Style this section like a **clean editorial table**.

Use:

* oversized section title
* rows divided by thin lines
* simple numbering
* minimal descriptive copy
* no colorful icons
* no service cards with shadows

This should resemble a modern visual index or table of contents.

---

### 8. Practice / Experience Section

Use a very minimal table layout.

It should feel like:

* understated
* elegant
* highly structured
* editorial
* typography-led

No corporate resume box styling.

---

### 9. Contact Section

The contact section should end the site in a bold but refined way.

Use:

* large monochrome statement
* minimal CTA button
* subtle contact details
* premium spacing
* calm closing composition

---

## Image Direction

Across the entire site, use imagery that feels:

* cinematic
* monochrome or desaturated
* fashion-inspired
* polished
* curated
* campaign-oriented
* social/content-related without looking like app screenshots everywhere

Use a combination of:

* campaign visuals
* social content mockups
* creator/content production scenes
* brand photoshoot style images
* subtle performance overlays
* carefully cropped visual storytelling images

Do **not** rely too heavily on dashboard screenshots.

---

## Typography Direction

Typography should drive the entire visual identity.

Use:

* oversized headings
* clean sans-serif fonts
* strong contrast in scale
* editorial line breaks
* uppercase micro labels
* subtle gray secondary text
* large visual rhythm between headlines and supporting content

Typography should feel closer to a **luxury portfolio** than a commercial landing page.

---

## CTA Style

All CTA buttons should be restyled globally.

Use only:

* outlined pill buttons
* monochrome styling
* subtle hover effects
* no bright filled buttons
* no aggressive call-to-action styling

Examples:

* View Campaigns
* Contact
* See Case Study

---

## Interaction Style

Use subtle interactions only:

* smooth scroll
* fade-in
* slide-up
* image zoom on hover
* soft reveal transitions

Avoid flashy startup animations or product-demo-like motion.

---

## Final Design Goal

The final website must look like a **premium editorial portfolio for a digital marketing professional**.

It should combine:

* the elegance of a high-end black-and-white creative portfolio
* the structure of a curated case study website
* the substance of a results-driven marketing portfolio

It should **not** look like:

* a SaaS homepage
* a digital agency landing page
* a startup marketing site
* an analytics dashboard

It should look like a **personal brand portfolio** with a refined visual language, where campaign work and numbers are presented through **editorial design, strong typography, and curated composition**.
